2. លំហសំណាក និងព្រឹត្តិការណ៍ (Sample Spaces and Events)

រំលឹក៖
  • ពិសោធន៍ (experiment) is a process whose outcome can be observed.
  • លំហសំណាក (sample space) $S$ or $\Omega$, of an experiment is the set of possible outcomes for the experiment. (The sample space may be finite or infinite).
  • ព្រឹត្តិការណ៍ (event) is a subset of the sample space, i.e. a (possibly empty) collection of possible outcomes to the experiment. We say that an event A OCCURS if the outcome is an element of $A$.

Note: The difference between an outcome and an event in this context is that an outcome is an individual possible result of the experiment; an event may include many different possible outcomes.

Experiment: Toss a coin three times.
  • The Sample Space is $$S=\Bigl\{{HHH}, {HHT}, {HTH}, {HTT}, {THH}, {THT}, {TTH}, {TTT}\Bigr\}$$
  • The event "2 heads, one tail" is the subset $$A=\{H H T, H T H, T H H\} \; \text{ of } S$$

  • Note that even when \(f\) is continuous in $\Omega$, a regular solution \(u\) may not exist in the space \(C^2(\Omega)\). For example, if we take the domain \((0, 1) \times (0, 1)\) and set \(f = 1\), the solution \(u\) cannot belong to \(C^2(\Omega)\) because the boundary conditions would imply certain values at the corners, leading to a contradiction with the equation.
  • This observation indicates that while \(f\) may be continuous, seeking a solution \(u\) in \(C^2(\Omega)\) is not always feasible. Instead, we have a better chance of finding a solution in a larger space, such as \(C^2(\Omega) \cap C^0(\Omega)\).
  • Thus, we are motivated to find an alternative formulation to the strong one, especially since the strong formulation does not adequately address cases with non-smooth data. In such scenarios, it is not guaranteed that the physical solution lies within the spaces \[C^2(\Omega) \cap C^0(\Omega)\quad \text{or}\quad C^1(\Omega) \cap C^0(\Omega)\]
