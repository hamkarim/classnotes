A partial order is a type of ordering of the elements of \(A\). Partial orders occur naturally in many areas of mathematics, including probability. A partial order on a set naturally gives rise to several other relations on the set.

ទំនាក់ទំនងលំដាប់ដោយផ្នែកលើសំណុំ \(A\) គឺជាទំនាក់ទំនង \(\preceq\) កំណត់លើ \(A\) ដែលមានលក្ខណៈខ្លួនឯង លក្ខណៈឆ្លុះស្មើ និងលក្ខណៈឆ្លង ។ គូ \((A, \preceq) \) ហៅថា សំណុំលំដាប់ដោយផ្នែក (Poset) ។ ដូច្នេះ គ្រប់ \( x, \ y, \ z \in A \) គេបាន :

  1. លក្ខណៈខ្លួនឯង: \( x \preceq x \)
  2. លក្ខណៈឆ្លុះស្មើ : បើ \( x \preceq y \) និង \( y \preceq x \) នោះគេបាន \( x = y \)
  3. លក្ខណៈឆ្លង : បើ \( x \preceq y \) និង \( y \preceq z \) នោះគេបាន \( x \preceq z \) ។

Extremal Elements

Suppose that \((A,\preceq)\) is a Poset and that \(X \subseteq A\).

  1. An element \(a \in A\) is the minimum element of \(A\) if and only if \(a \preceq x\) for every \(x \in A\).
  2. An element \(a \in A\) is a minimal element of \(A\) if and only if no \(x \in A\) satisfies \(x \prec a\).
  3. An element \(b \in A\) is the maximum element of \(A\) if and only if \(b\succeq x\) for every \(x \in A\).
  4. An element \(b \in A\) is a maximal element of \(A\) if and only if no \(x \in A\) satisfies \(x \succ b\).
Remarks:
  • In general, a set can have several maximal and minimal elements (or none).
  • On the other hand, The minimum and maximum elements of \(A\), if they exist, are unique. They are denoted \(\min(A)\) and \(\max(A)\), respectively.
    • Proof

    Suppose that \( a, \ b \) are minimum elements of \( A \). Since \( a, \ b \in A \) we have \( a \preceq b \) and \( b \preceq a \), so \( a = b \) by the antisymmetric property. The proof for the maximum element is analogous.

  • Minimal, maximal, minimum, and maximum elements of a set must belong to that set. The following definitions relate to upper and lower bounds of a set, which do not have to belong to the set.

Suppose that \((A, \preceq )\) is a Poset and that \(X \subseteq A \). Then

  1. An element \(u \in A\) is a lower bound for \(X\) if and only if \(u \preceq x\) for every \(x \in X\).
  2. An element \(v \in A\) is an upper bound for \(X\) if and only if \(v \succeq x\) for every \(x \in X\).
  3. The greatest lower bound or infimum of \(X\), if it exists, is the maximum of the set of lower bounds of \(X\).
  4. The least upper bound or supremum of \(X\), if it exists, is the minimum of the set of upper bounds of \(X\).

Remarks:

  • The greatest lower bound of \(X\) is unique, if it exists. It is denoted \(\text{glb}(X)\) or \(\inf(X)\). Similarly, the least upper bound of \(X\) is unique, if it exists, and is denoted \(\text{lub}(X)\) or \(\sup(X)\).
  • Note that every element of \(X\) is a lower bound and an upper bound for \(\varnothing\), since the conditions in the definition hold vacuously.
  • The symbols \( \wedge \) and \( \vee \) are also used for infimum and supremum, respectively, so \[ \bigwedge X = \inf(X) \text{ , } \quad \bigvee X = \sup(X) \] if they exist.
  • In particular, for \( x, \ y \in A \), operator notation is more commonly used, so \[ x \wedge y = \inf\{x, y\} \text{ , } \quad x \vee y = \sup\{x, y\} \] Partially ordered sets for which these elements always exist are important, and have a special name.
  • A Poset \((A, \preceq)\) is called a lattice if \( x \wedge y \) and \( x \vee y \) exist for every \( x, \ y \in A \).

For the subset partial order, the $\inf$ and $\sup$ operators correspond to intersection and union, respectively:

Proposition. Let \(X\) be a set and consider the POR \(\subseteq\) on \(A=\mathscr{P}(X)\), the power set of \(X\). Let \(\mathcal{F}\) be a nonempty subset of \(\mathscr{P}(X)\), that is, a nonempty collection of subsets of \(X\). Then
  1. \(\inf(\mathcal{F}) = \bigcap \mathcal{F}\)
  2. \(\sup(\mathcal{F}) = \bigcup \mathcal{F}\)
