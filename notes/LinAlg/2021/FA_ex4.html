Bounded Operator

Problem1 : (Equivalent Norm)

Let $X$ be linear space. Suppose $\|\cdot\|_a$ and $\|\cdot\|_b$ are equivalent in $X$. Show that such property is unchanged on the space $\mathcal{B}(X)$ (space of bounded linear operators on $X$).

HINT: Derive from the two equivalent norms on a normed linear space $X$.

Show solution

No way!

Problem 2 : (Bounded Operator)

Let $X=\left(C([0,1]),\|\cdot\|_{\infty}\right)$. We define a linear operator on $X$ by $$ (A \phi )(x):=\int_{0}^{1} a(x, y) \phi(y) \d y $$ where the scalare field $a:[0,1] \times[0,1] \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ is continuous. Can the operator $A$ be bounded?

HINT: With $\|a\|_{\infty} \lt +\infty$, can you approximate $\|A \phi\|_{\infty}$?

Show solution

No way!

Problem 3 : (Norm on complex polynomial)

Let $X = P[\mathbb C]$ , and define the norm $\alpha$ on $X$ by $$\|p\|_{\alpha}:=\sum_{k=0}^{n}(k+1)\left|a_{k}\right|$$ for every $p \in X$ where $p(x)=\sum_{k=0}^{n} a_{k} x^{k}$.

  1. Show that $\|\cdot \|_{\alpha}$ is a norm on $X$.
  2. Show that $\left(X,\|\cdot\|_{\alpha}\right)$ is not complete.
  3. Given a linear functional $$T: X \rightarrow \mathbb{C}\quad \text{ by } \quad T(p):= p^{\prime}(1)$$ When $X$ is equipped with $\|\cdot\|_{\alpha}$, compute the norm of $T$?
  4. If $X$ is equipped with $\|\cdot\|_{\calC([0,1])}$ where we define $$\|p\|_{\calC([0,1])} := \sup _{x \in[0,1]}|p(x)|, \quad p \in X$$ then compute the norm of $T$ above?
  5. Are $\|\cdot \|_{\alpha}$ and $\|\cdot\|_{\calC([0,1])}$ equivalent norms on $X$?

HINT:

Show solution

No way!

Problem 4 : (Norm of sequences that converge)

Consider $c_{0}:=\left\{x \in \mathbb{K}^{\mathbb{N}} : \lim _{n \rightarrow+\infty} x(n)=0\right\}$ (set of sequences that converge to $0$). We define a function $$T: \ell^{1} \rightarrow c_{0}^{*} \quad \text{ by } \quad \big(T x\big)(y) := \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} x_{k} y_{k}$$ Show that $T$ is homeomorphic.

HINT:

Show solution

No way!

Problem 5 : (Banach Space)

For $m\geqslant 0$, consider a normed $$ \|f\|_{m}:=\sup _{t \geqslant 0} \mathrm{e}^{m t}|f(t)|\lt +\infty . $$ where $\mathcal{C}_{m}$ is the space of continuous functions $f:[0, \infty) \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$.

  1. Show that $\mathcal{C}_{m}$ is a Banach space, equipped with the above norm.
  2. Let $m, n \geqslant 0$ and $\phi \in \mathcal{C}_{n}$. We define on operator $T$ by $$T f=f \cdot \phi$$ Show that the operator $T\in \mathscr{L}\left(\mathcal{C}_{m}, \mathcal{C}_{m+n}\right)$. And what is $\|T\|$?
  3. Let $0 \leqslant m \lt n$ and define $A: \mathcal{C}_{m} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}_{m}$ by $$ A f(t)=\int_{0}^{t} \mathrm{e}^{-n (t-s)} f(s) \mathrm{d} s $$ Prove that $\|As\|=(\beta-\alpha)^{-1}$

HINT:

Show solution

No way!
