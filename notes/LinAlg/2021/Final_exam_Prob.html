  លំហាត់ជ្រើសរើសពហុចម្លើយ.

  1. Two boxes containing balls are placed on a table. The boxes are labelled $B_1$ and $B_2$. Box $B_1$ contains 7 white balls and 4 black. Box $B_2$ contains 3 white balls and 10 blue. The boxes are arranged so that the probability of selecting box $B_1$ is $1 / 3$ and the probability of selecting box $B_2$ is $2 / 3$. Dara is blindfolded (បិទភ្នែក) and asked to select a ball. He will win a colour TV if he selects a white. What is the probability that Dara will win the TV (that is, he will select a white ball)?
    1. $7 / 33$
    2. $6 / 33$
    3. $13 / 33$
    4. $20 / 33$
  2. A box contains an mango, a pear, a plum, and a banana. How many different pairs of fruit can be selected from the box?
    1. ${ }_4 P_2$
    2. ${ }_2 P_4$
    3. ${ }_4 C_2$
    4. ${ }_2 C_4$
  3. The process of unlocking a combination lock that has a dial (គ្រាប់បង្វិល) with 60 numbers. To open the lock, you need to select and input three specific numbers in a precise order: first turning the dial to the right, then to the left, and finally back to the right again. See explaination:
    • Combination Lock: A security device that requires a specific sequence of numbers to open.
    • 60 Numbers: The range of numbers available on the lock's dial (from 0 to 59).
    • Turning Sequence: The required action of turning the dial three times in the specified order to input the combination.
    • 3 Different Selected Numbers: You need to choose three distinct numbers out of the 60 available to unlock the device.
    The selection of these 3 numbers is an example of $---$
    1. a permutation.
    2. a combination.
    3. both a combination and a permutation.
    4. neither a combination nor a permutation.
  4. Piseth and Dara play a tennis match. The first person to win 2 games wins the match. In how many different ways can a winner be determined?
    1. $3$
    2. $5$
    3. 6
    4. 8
  5. តើលេខ 6 ខ្ទង់ដែលធំជាង $800000$ អាចត្រូវបានបង្កើត មានចំនួនប៉ុន្មាន យកចេញពីលេខ $1,1,5,5,5,8$?
    1. $10$
    2. $60$
    3. $64$
    4. $120$
  6. In how many ways can four pepsi, three iced teas, and three orange juices be distributed among ten students if each graduate is to receive one beverage?
    1. $36$
    2. $4200$
    3. $604800$
    4. $3628800$
  7. Solve for $n:{ }_n P_2=42$
    1. $2$
    2. 6
    3. 7
    4. 42
  8. Assuming that at least one coin is used, how many different sums of money can be made from the following coins: a penny, a nickel, a dime, a quarter, and a dollar?
    1. 16
    2. 31
    3. 32
    4. 120
  9. How many different committees of $2$ people can be selected from $5$ people?
    1. $\frac{5!}{2!}$
    2. $\frac{5!}{3!}$
    3. $\frac{5!}{2!3!}$
    4. $5!$
  10. When you play lotto, you must choose 5 different integers from 1 to 30 . How many combinations are possible?
    1. $\frac{30!}{5!25!}$
    2. $\frac{30!}{25!}$
    3. $25!$
    4. $\frac{30!}{5!}$
  11. $M$ and $N$ are two events such that $P(\overline{M})=0.4$ and $P(M \cap N)=0.2$ Then $P(M \cap \overline{N})$ is equal to $------$
    1. $0.4$
    2. $0.2$
    3. $0.6$
    4. $0.8 $
  12. A problem in mathematics is given to three students $A, B$ and $C$. If the probability of $A$ solving the problem is $1 / 2$ and $B$ not solving it is $1 / 4$. The whole probability of the problem being solved is $63 / 64$, then what is the probability of solving it?
    1. $1 / 8$
    2. $1 / 64$
    3. $7 / 8$
    4. $1 / 2$
  13. If $A$ and $B$ are two mutually exclusive events with $P(\overline{A})=5 / 6$ and $P(B)=1 / 3$ then $P(A|\overline{B})$ is equal to $----$
    1. $1 / 4$
    2. $1 / 2$
    3. $0$ , since mutually exclusive
    4. $5 / 18$
  14. If $A$ and $B$ are two events such that $P(A)=0.2, P(B)=0.6$ and $P(A | B)=0.2$, then the value of $P(A | \overline{B})$ is $----$
    1. $0.2$
    2. $0.5$
    3. $0.8$
    4. $1 / 3$
  15. If $A$ and $B$ are two mutually exclusive events with $P(A)\gt 0$ and $P(B)\gt 0$, then it implies they are also independent.
    1. True
    2. False
  16. What is the probability of an impossible event?
    1. $0$
    2. $1$
    3. Not defined
    4. Insufficient data
  17. If $A=A_1 \cup A_2 \ldots \ldots . . . \cup A_n$, where $A_1 \ldots A_n$ are mutually exclusive events then?
    1. $\sum_{i=0}^n P\left(A_i\right)$
    2. $\sum_{i=1}^n P\left(A_i\right)$
    3. $\prod_{i=0}^n P\left(A_i\right)$
    4. Not defined
  18. A problem in algebra is given to three students $A, B$ and $C$. Their probabilities of solving the problem are $\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{3}$ and $\frac{1}{4}$ respectively. If all of them try independently, then the probability, that problem is solved, is
    1. $\frac{2}{3}$
    2. $\frac{3}{4}$
    3. $\frac{1}{3}$
    4. $\frac{1}{4}$
  19. Three numbers are chosen at random without replacement from $\{1,2,3, \ldots, 8\}$. The probability that their minimum is 3 , given that their maximum is 6 , is:
    1. $\frac{3}{8}$
    2. $\frac{1}{5}$
    3. $\frac{1}{4}$
    4. $\frac{2}{5}$
  20. A multiple choice examination has 5 questions. Each question has three alternative answers of which exactly one is correct. The probability that a student will get 4 or more correct answers just by guessing is
    1. $\frac{13}{3^5}$
    2. $\frac{11}{3^5}$
    3. $\frac{10}{3^5}$
    4. $\frac{17}{3^5}$
  21. If $12$ distinct balls are to be placed in $3$ identical boxes, then the probability that one of the boxes contains exactly 3 balls is
    1. $\frac{55}{3}\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{11}$
    2. $55\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{11}$
    3. $22\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{11}$
    4. $228\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{12}$
  22. Three companies $A, B$ and $C$ supply $25$%, $35 $% and $40$% of the notebooks to a school. Past experience shows that $5$%, $4$% and $2$% of the notebooks produced by these companies are defective. If a notebook was found to be defective, what is the probability that the notebook was supplied by $A$?
    1. $44 / 69$
    2. $25 / 69$
    3. $13 / 24$
    4. $11 / 24$
  23. ប្រអប់មួយផ្ទុកទឹកព្រីន (cartridges) ចំនួន 30 ដែលមានចំនួន 6 ខូច ។ If 3 of the cartridges are removed from the box in succession without replacement, what is the probability that all the 3 cartridges are defective?
    1. $\frac{(6 * 5 * 4)}{(30 * 30 * 30)}$
    2. $\frac{(6 * 5 * 4)}{(30 * 29 * 28)}$
    3. $\frac{(6 * 5 * 3)}{(30 * 29 * 28)}$
    4. $\frac{(6 * 6 * 6)}{(30 * 30 * 30)}$
  24. Suppose box $A$ contains 4 red and 5 blue coins and box $B$ contains 6 red and 3 blue coins. $A$ coin is chosen at random from the box $A$ and placed in box $B$. Finally, a coin is chosen at random from among those now in box $B$. What is the probability a blue coin was transferred from box $A$ to box $B$ given that the coin chosen from box $B$ is red?
    1. $15 / 29$
    2. $14 / 29$
    3. $1 / 2$
    4. $7 / 10$
  25. ប្រអប់ $B_1$ មួយមានឃ្លីព័ណ៌សចំនួន 2 និងខ្មៅចំនួន 3 ចំណែកប្រអប់ $B_2$ មានឃ្លីសចំនួន 3 និងខ្មៅចំនួន 4 ។ ប្រអប់មួយត្រូវបានចាប់យកដោយចៃដន្យ និងគេចាប់យកឃ្លីមួយពីក្នុងប្រអប់នោះ ។ If the ball drawn is found black, find the probability that the ball chosen was $\mathrm{B}_1$.
    1. $4 / 7$
    2. $3 / 7$
    3. $20 / 41$
    4. $21 / 41$
  26. នៅសាកលវិទ្យាល័យមួយ $4$% នៃនិស្សិតប្រុស មានកម្ពស់លើសពី 6 feet និង $1$% នៃនិស្សិតស្រីមានកំពស់លើសពី 6 feet ។ ចំនួននិស្សិតសរុបមានសមាមាត្រស្ត្រី 3 និងបុរស 2 ។ បើនិស្សិតម្នាក់ត្រូវបានជ្រើសរើសដោយចៃដន្យ ពីក្នុងចំណោមអ្នកដែលមានកម្ពស់លើសពី 6 feet នោះតើប្រូបាប៊ីលីតេដែលនិស្សិតនោះជាស្ត្រី ស្មើប៉ុន្មាន?
    1. $2 / 5$
    2. $3 / 5$
    3. $3 / 11$
    4. $1 / 100$
  27. ឧបមាថា គេជ្រើសរើសបុរស $5$ ពីក្នុងចំណោមបុរស $100$ និងរើសស្ត្រី $10$ ពីក្នុងចំណោមស្ត្រីចំនួន $250$ ដែលមានជំងឺងងឹតព័ណ៌ ។ ចូររកប្រូបាបប៊ីលីតេសរុប ចំពោះអ្នកមានជំងឺងងឹតព័ណ៌។ (numbers of men and women are equal.)
    1. $0.45$
    2. $0.045$
    3. $0.05$
    4. $0.5$
  28. ក្នុងថ្នាក់មួយ ក្មេងប្រុស $40$% ជ្រើសរើសយកគណិតវិទ្យា និងក្មេងស្រី $60$% ជ្រើសរើសយកគណិតវិទ្យា។ តើប្រូបាប៊ីលីតេដែលគណិតវិទ្យាត្រូវបានជ្រើសរើសស្មើប៉ុន្មាន បើពាក់កណ្តាលនៃចំនួនសិស្សក្នុងថ្នាក់គឺជាក្មេងស្រី?
